Why We Gather

Community care is part of the work.

At L.A.U.R.A. (Life After Uncivil Ruthless Acts), community events are a vital part of our mission to enhance the quality of life in South Central Los Angeles.

Through weekly meetings, monthly gatherings, and annual events, we empower youth, support victim-survivors and families, and strengthen community collaboration because lasting change happens when communities come together.

Annual Peace Walk & Community Resource Fair

Walk for Peace. Rally for Change. Build Community.

Each March, L.A.U.R.A. brings South Central Los Angeles together for its signature event, the Annual Peace Walk & Community Resource Fair. This free, family-centered gathering honors lives lost to violence while mobilizing our community around healing, prevention, and hope.

Location

Fred Roberts Park

South Central Los Angeles

When

Annually in March

Cost

Free and open to the public

Event Impact

  • 17+ years of community impact
  • 1,000+ attendees annually
  • Dozens of community partners and resource providers
  • Hundreds of families supported each year

Together, we are building a culture of peace, care, and opportunity.

Why We Walk

Grief becomes action. Remembrance becomes resilience.

The Peace Walk begins our day together, uniting families, survivors, youth, and community members in one collective voice to say: End Violence. Choose Peace.

We walk alongside families whose loved ones have been murdered, honoring their lives while affirming our shared commitment to creating safer, healthier communities.

L.A.U.R.A.'s long-term vision is to grow the Peace Walk into a Community 5K for Peace.

The Community Resource Fair

After the walk, Fred Roberts Park becomes a vibrant hub of connection, culture, and care.

  • Health, wellness, and social service resources
  • Community-based organizations and local vendors
  • Youth activities and family-friendly programming
  • Live entertainment and special guest appearances
  • Food, raffles, prizes, and giveaways
