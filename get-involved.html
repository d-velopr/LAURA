L.A.U.R.A Youth Program

L.A.U.R.A. Youth is a community-centered program dedicated to supporting, empowering, and uplifting the next generation in South Central Los Angeles. Through prevention, intervention, mentorship, and leadership opportunities, we create safe spaces where youth can heal, grow, and build positive futures.

Our program encourages youth to lead with purpose through community events, youth forums, workshops, outreach, and peer support while developing confidence, civic awareness, leadership skills, and long-term goals for their future.

Youth Awards

Scholarships

L.A.U.R.A. supports youth through scholarships that help students pursue their educational goals and future opportunities. By investing in students, L.A.U.R.A. helps create pathways to learning, growth, and future success.

Civic Engagement Recognition Award

L.A.U.R.A. honors two youth leaders each year with the Civic Engagement Recognition Award at our annual March community fair, recognizing their leadership and positive impact within the community.

Ways to Volunteer

Give your time. Support the community. Find a role that fits you.

Become a Youth Leader

Whether you want to mentor, help plan events, support community outreach, or bring new ideas to the next generation, we invite you to grow with us and be part of creating lasting change.

Help at Events

Support our resource fairs, holiday drives, and community gatherings. Event volunteers are the backbone of what we do.

Workshops & Skills

A professional, artist, or tradesperson? Lead a workshop. Our youth want to learn from people doing real work in the world.

Photography & Media

Help us document our community's story. Photographers, videographers, and social media creators are always welcome.

Translation & Outreach

Bilingual? Help us connect with Spanish-speaking families who need support and ensure no one is left out due to a language barrier.

Something Else?

Have a skill or idea that doesn't fit the boxes above? Reach out — we'll find a way to put your energy to work.
